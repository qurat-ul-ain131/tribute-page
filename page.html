Hazrat Muhammad (ﷺ)

The Prophet Who Changed the Course of Human History

Here's a timeline of the Prophet's life:

  • 570 CE – Born in Makkah (Mecca), in the tribe of Quraysh.
  • 576 CE – Loses his mother, Amina, at a young age and is later raised by his grandfather and then his uncle, Abu Talib.
  • 595 CE – Marries Hazrat Khadijah (RA), a respected businesswoman. Their marriage becomes a source of love, support, and stability.
  • 610 CE – Receives the first revelation from Allah through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) in the Cave of Hira. The mission of Prophethood begins.
  • 613 CE – Publicly starts calling people to worship one God (Allah) and to abandon idolatry.
  • 615 CE – Some of his followers migrate to Abyssinia (Ethiopia) to escape persecution.
  • 619 CE – Experiences the "Year of Sorrow" with the deaths of his wife Khadijah (RA) and his uncle Abu Talib.
  • 620 CE – The miraculous journey of Isra and Mi'raj takes place.
  • 622 CE – Migrates from Makkah to Madinah. This event, known as the Hijrah, marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
  • 624 CE – The Battle of Badr takes place, a significant moment in early Islamic history.
  • 625 CE – The Battle of Uhud occurs.
  • 627 CE – The Battle of the Trench (Khandaq) helps protect Madinah from a large coalition of enemies.
  • 628 CE – The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah is signed, paving the way for greater peace and the spread of Islam.
  • 630 CE – Returns to Makkah with his followers and peacefully reclaims the city. He forgives many of his former enemies.
  • 632 CE – Delivers the Farewell Sermon, emphasizing equality, justice, and the rights of all people.
  • 632 CE – Passes away in Madinah at the age of 63.
